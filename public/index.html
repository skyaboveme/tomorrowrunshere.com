Chapter Five · The Real Math

A billion-dollar taxpayer that asks for almost nothing back.

Here's the real math behind growth. A 1,000-home subdivision adds roughly 1,500 students to the school district, daily traffic, and heavy demand on water and emergency services. A billion-dollar data center adds a massive taxable asset and zero new students — so 100% of its new school revenue goes to teacher pay, modern facilities, and technology. The commercial tax base shoulders the county's burden so rising property taxes don't fall on hard-working families.

Chapter Six · The Loop

The water stays in the loop.

Modern campuses don't cool like the old evaporative towers that consumed water continuously. They run a sealed, closed loop: coolant absorbs heat directly at the chip, a heat exchanger rejects it without ever mixing fluids, and the same water recirculates over and over — no evaporation, no constant intake of fresh water. At the newest West Texas campuses, chip-level liquid cooling means the restrooms use more water than the cooling system does.

Chapter Seven · The Bastrop Story

It's already happening, minutes east of Austin.

EdgeConneX is building a $1.44 billion campus in Bastrop County: four buildings across 140 acres near Cedar Creek, with the $440 million first structure alone spanning 578,000 square feet. It joins a cluster that includes Tesla, xAI, Starlink, The Boring Company, and Neuralink — while a $43 million grant delivers countywide gigafiber to every resident. Historic downtowns and pine forests on one side. A global center of innovation on the other. Both, on purpose.
